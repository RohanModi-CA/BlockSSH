Round 4 goal
- Move from spot checks to a catalog-level table over the hand-picked 0681 peak list.
- Evaluate every listed peak with the same bond-agnostic mode-likeness machinery.
- Combine two evidence channels:
  - mode-like recurrence across views
  - child-like evidence from the first-pass taxonomy

Why this round matters
- The free scans are useful, but they still require reading score curves by eye.
- The curated peak list is the right place to start building a machine-readable ranking of:
  - peaks that look consistently mode-like
  - peaks that are strong only in one view
  - peaks that already have a plausible lower-frequency explanation

Planned outputs
- A catalog CSV with one row per listed peak.
- For each peak:
  - best-scoring view
  - top-view and median-view mode score
  - recurrence counts across datasets/components
  - whether the peak sits inside a detected family in each view
  - child-evidence from the taxonomy pass
  - a combined “fundamental-likeness” score
- Diagnostic plots for:
  - ranking peaks by combined evidence
  - child evidence vs recurrence
  - known reference neighborhoods

What this means
- The bond-agnostic family scan helped the `16.6` neighborhood, but the full catalog table says it is still not as broadly recurrent or as cleanly unexplained as the strongest fundamentals.
- That does not prove it is not fundamental. It does mean the current evidence stack is still missing something for that line.
- Likely missing pieces:
  - allowing family-centered evaluation instead of only exact listed-peak evaluation
  - using a narrower local window around high-frequency candidates
  - allowing observable-specific evidence instead of demanding broad recurrence too strongly
