Round 8 goal
- Separate two different questions that were still being mixed together:
  - does a line look mode-like in recurrence/scaling?
  - does it also have a specific reproducible parent relation?

Main lesson entering this round
- High-frequency recurrence alone is not enough to call something fundamental.
- `18.36 Hz` can still look moderately recurrent under adaptive windows.
- What makes `18` different is not just recurrence; it is that a very specific parent explanation survives targeted null tests in the right observable.

New discriminator to build
- For each high-frequency candidate:
  - mode-likeness score from adaptive recurrence
  - parent-consistency score from targeted null-based parent tests
- A likely fundamental should have:
  - moderate-to-strong mode-likeness
  - weak or inconsistent parent-consistency
- A likely child/follower should have:
  - moderate mode-likeness can still happen
  - but parent-consistency should be strong and specific

Update after running the discriminator

Interpretation
- This is the cleanest conceptual separation so far:
  - `18` can look somewhat recurrent under adaptive scaling, but it also has a much sharper parent explanation
  - `16.6` currently looks more like a line with moderate recurrence and only weak / inconsistent parent explanations
- So recurrence alone is not enough, and that was one of the hidden conceptual errors in earlier attempts.
- The right discriminator for higher lines appears to be:
  - recurrence / scaling evidence
  - plus specificity of the best parent explanation
