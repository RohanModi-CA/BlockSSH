Round 9 goal
- Build a two-axis frontier for the listed 10-20 Hz peaks.

Axes
- x-axis:
  - high-frequency mode-likeness from the adaptive-window catalog
- y-axis:
  - parent-specificity from the refined catalog
  - this is the weighted child penalty, not raw child evidence

Why this matters
- A peak can be mode-like and still be a follower.
- A peak can also be weakly explained by some lower-frequency arithmetic without that explanation being convincing.
- The right picture is therefore:
  - strong mode-likeness + low parent-specificity = more fundamental-like
  - strong mode-likeness + high parent-specificity = more follower-like
  - low mode-likeness = ambiguous or weak, regardless of parent score
